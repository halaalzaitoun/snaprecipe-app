# SnapRecipe

Turn saved recipes into something you’ll actually use.

---

## Overview

SnapRecipe is a product designed to help users turn messy recipe content from screenshots, social media, and notes into a clean, searchable recipe library.

It focuses on transforming unstructured content into structured, usable data so users can easily find, organize, and cook recipes they’ve saved.

---

## 🎯 Problem

Recipes today are scattered across multiple sources:

- Screenshots saved on phones  
- Instagram posts and reels  
- Notes and bookmarks  

This creates several challenges:

- Recipes are hard to find later  
- Instructions are not easy to follow  
- Content is unstructured and inconsistent  
- Nutritional information is often missing  

As a result, many saved recipes are never actually used.

---

## 💡 Solution

SnapRecipe transforms unstructured recipe content into clean, structured, and usable recipes.

Users can:

- Import recipes from screenshots, links, or text  
- Automatically extract ingredients and instructions  
- Review and edit parsed content  
- See estimated calories and macros  
- Save and organize recipes into collections  
- Search and quickly find recipes based on ingredients, tags, or keywords  

The goal is to turn recipe inspiration into something practical, searchable, and reusable.

---

## 👥 Target Users

- Home cooks who save recipes but rarely use them  
- Users who collect recipes from social media  
- People looking to organize and reuse cooking content  
- Health-conscious users interested in nutritional insights  

---

## 🔄 Core User Journey

1. User imports a recipe (image, link, or text)  
2. App parses unstructured content into structured data  
3. User reviews and edits extracted ingredients and steps  
4. Recipe is saved to a personal library  
5. User organizes recipes into collections and filters  
6. User accesses recipes easily while cooking  

---

## ✨ MVP Features

- Recipe import (image, link, or text)  
- Parsing and review flow  
- Editable recipe details  
- Nutrition summary (mock)  
- Recipe library with filters  
- Collections for organization  

---

## ⚖️ Product Decisions and Tradeoffs

- Focused on **import and structuring** rather than full meal planning  
- Used simplified parsing logic to prioritize speed over perfect accuracy  
- Included nutrition as an estimate to demonstrate value without heavy complexity  
- Prioritized editing flow to build user trust in extracted data  
- Limited scope to validate the core loop: capture → structure → use
